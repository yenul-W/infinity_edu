Section 1

GST & Percentage Change

Q1 2004 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Rita purchased a camera for $880 while on holidays in Australia. This price included 10% GST. When she left Australia she received a refund of the GST. What was Rita's refund?

  • A. $80
  • B. $88
  • C. $792
  • D. $800
Show solution

The $880 already includes 10% GST, so it equals 110% of the pre-GST price.

GST = $880 × 10 ÷ 110 = $880 ÷ 11 = $80 → Answer: A
Q2 2010 HSC ↗ 1 mark

A new phone was purchased for $725 which included 10% GST. What was the price of the phone without GST, correct to the nearest cent?

  • A. $65.91
  • B. $72.50
  • C. $652.50
  • D. $659.09
Show solution
Price without GST = $725 ÷ 1.10 = $659.09 → Answer: D
Q3 2007 HSC ↗ 1 mark

The price of a CD is $22.00, which includes 10% GST. What is the amount of GST included in this price?

  • A. $2.00
  • B. $2.20
  • C. $19.80
  • D. $20.00
Show solution
GST = $22 × 10 ÷ 110 = $22 ÷ 11 = $2.00 → Answer: A
Q4 SM-Bank 2 marks

A dress was on sale with 25% discount. As a regular customer, Kate received a further 10% on the already discounted price. What was the overall percentage discount Kate received?

Show solution
After 25% off → 75% of original price remains After further 10% off → 90% × 75% = 67.5% Overall discount = 100% − 67.5% = 32.5%
Q5 SM-Bank 3 marks

A golf shop is having a Boxing Day sale:

(i) What is the percentage discount on a putter which is reduced from $120.00 to $102.00? [1 mark]

(ii) The same discount applies storewide. What discount amount is applicable to a box of golf balls whose original price was $25.00? [1 mark]

(iii) What is the sale price of a golf bag which originally cost $160.00? [1 mark]

Show solution
(i) Discount = ($120 − $102) ÷ $120 × 100 = $18 ÷ $120 × 100 = 15% (ii) Discount = 15% × $25 = $3.75 (iii) Sale price = $160 × (1 − 0.15) = $160 × 0.85 = $136.00
Q6 2023 HSC ↗ 3 marks

A receipt from a supermarket shows a total of $124.87. The GST shown on the receipt is $3.86. GST, at a rate of 10%, is only charged on some items. What was the value of the items which did NOT have GST charged?

Show solution
Value of GST items (including GST) = $3.86 × 11 = $42.46 Value without GST = $124.87 − $42.46 = $82.41

Section 2

Budgeting

Q7 2005 HSC ↗ 5 marks

Reece is preparing his annual budget. His expected income is: $90 every week as a swimming coach, plus interest earned from an investment of $5000 at a rate of 4% per annum. His planned expenses are: $30 every week on transport, $12 every week on lunches, $48 every month on entertainment. Reece will save his remaining income. (Assume 52 weeks in a year.)

(i) Determine Reece's annual income, annual expenses, and annual savings. [3 marks]

(ii) At the beginning of the year, Reece starts saving. Will Reece have saved enough money during the year for a deposit of $2100 on a car if he keeps to his budget? Justify your answer. [2 marks]

Show solution
(i) Annual income = (90 × 52) + (5000 × 4%) = $4680 + $200 = $4880 Annual expenses = (30 × 52) + (12 × 52) + (48 × 12) = $1560 + $624 + $576 = $2760 Annual savings = $4880 − $2760 = $2120 (ii) $2120 ≥ $2100, so YES, Reece will have just enough for the deposit. He will save $2120 during the year, which exceeds the $2100 deposit.
Q8 2021 HSC ↗ 3 marks

A dance school runs a holiday program and charges $150 per student. The costs of running the program are: Dance teacher: $110 per hour; Room hire: $52 per hour plus 10% GST. The program goes for two hours a day for five days. What profit does the dance school make if 20 students pay for the program?

Show solution
Revenue = 20 × $150 = $3000 Total hours = 2 × 5 = 10 hours Teacher cost = 10 × $110 = $1100 Room hire = 10 × $52 × 1.10 = $572 (including GST) Total costs = $1100 + $572 = $1672 Profit = $3000 − $1672 = $1328

Insurance & Vehicle Costs

Q9 2019 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Julia earns $28 per hour. Her hourly pay rate increases by 2%. How much will she earn for a 4-hour shift with this increase?

  • A. $2.24
  • B. $28.56
  • C. $112
  • D. $114.24
Show solution
New rate = $28 × 1.02 = $28.56/hr Earnings = $28.56 × 4 = $114.24 → Answer: D
Q10 2018 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Sam is the driver at fault in a car accident. Which of the following is covered by Sam's compulsory third-party (CTP) insurance?

  • A. Repairs to Sam's car
  • B. Injury to the other driver
  • C. Damage to the other driver's car
  • D. Cost of repairing a building damaged in the accident
Show solution

CTP (Green Slip) insurance covers personal injury only — it pays for medical costs and compensation for people injured in the accident. It does not cover property damage.

Answer: B
Q11 2019 HSC ↗ 2 marks

Ralph buys a utility vehicle with a market value of $63,500. Stamp duty is calculated as: 3% of market value up to $45,000, and 5% of market value over $45,000. Calculate the amount of stamp duty payable by Ralph.

Show solution
Stamp duty on first $45,000 = 3% × $45,000 = $1350 Amount over $45,000 = $63,500 − $45,000 = $18,500 Stamp duty on remainder = 5% × $18,500 = $925 Total stamp duty = $1350 + $925 = $2275
Q12 SM-Bank 1 mark

Alex is buying a used car with a sale price of $13,380. Stamp duty is calculated at $3 for every $100, or part thereof, of the sale price. Calculate the stamp duty payable.

Show solution
$13,380 ÷ $100 = 133.8 → round UP to 134 (because of "or part thereof") Stamp duty = 134 × $3 = $402

Section 4

Simple Interest

Q13 SM-Bank 1 mark

$6000 is invested in an account that earns simple interest at the rate of 3.5% per annum. What is the total interest earned in the first four years?

  • A. $70
  • B. $84
  • C. $210
  • D. $840
Show solution
I = P × R × N = $6000 × 0.035 × 4 = $840 → Answer: D
Q14 2023 HSC ↗ 1 mark

An amount of $2500 is invested at a simple interest rate of 3% per annum. How much interest is earned in the first two years?

  • A. $75
  • B. $150
  • C. $2575
  • D. $2652
Show solution
I = $2500 × 0.03 × 2 = $150 → Answer: B
Q15 2010 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Minjy invests $2000 for 1 year and 5 months. The simple interest is calculated at a rate of 6% per annum. What is the total value of the investment at the end of this period?

  • A. $2170
  • B. $2180
  • C. $3003
  • D. $3700
Show solution
N = 1 + 5/12 = 17/12 years I = $2000 × 0.06 × 17/12 = $2000 × 0.085 = $170 Total = $2000 + $170 = $2170 → Answer: A
Q16 2013 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Polly borrowed $11,000. She repaid the loan in full at the end of two years with a lump sum of $12,000. What annual simple interest rate was she charged?

  • A. 4.17%
  • B. 4.55%
  • C. 8.33%
  • D. 9.09%
Show solution
Interest = $12,000 − $11,000 = $1000 R = I ÷ (P × N) = $1000 ÷ ($11,000 × 2) = 1000 ÷ 22000 ≈ 4.55% p.a. → Answer: B
Q17 2009 HSC ↗ 2 marks

Lou bought a plasma TV which was priced at $3499. He paid $1000 deposit and got a loan for the balance that was paid off by 24 monthly instalments of $135.36. What simple interest rate per annum, to the nearest percent, was charged on his loan?

Show solution
Principal (loan) = $3499 − $1000 = $2499 Total repaid = 24 × $135.36 = $3248.64 Total interest = $3248.64 − $2499 = $749.64 N = 24 months = 2 years R = $749.64 ÷ ($2499 × 2) ≈ 0.15 ≈ 15% p.a.
Q18 SM-Bank 2 marks

George makes a single deposit of $9000 into an account that pays simple interest. After 4 years, George's account has a balance of $10,350. What simple interest rate did George receive on his investment?

Show solution
Interest earned = $10,350 − $9000 = $1350 R = I ÷ (P × N) = $1350 ÷ ($9000 × 4) = 3.75% p.a.

Section 5

Straight-Line Depreciation

Q19 2023 HSC ↗ 1 mark

A delivery truck was valued at $65,000 when new. The value of the truck depreciates at a rate of 22 cents per kilometre travelled. What is the value of the truck after it has travelled a total distance of 132,600 km?

  • A. $35,828
  • B. $29,172
  • C. $14,872
  • D. $14,300
Show solution
Total depreciation = $0.22 × 132,600 = $29,172 Value = $65,000 − $29,172 = $35,828 → Answer: A
Q20 2013 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Rae paid $40,000 for new office equipment at the start of the 2013 financial year. She used flat rate depreciation each year. At the start of the 2016 financial year she revalued her equipment at $22,000. What was the annual flat rate of depreciation, as a percentage of the purchase price?

  • A. 11.25%
  • B. 15%
  • C. 17.5%
  • D. 35%
Show solution
Depreciation over 3 years = $40,000 − $22,000 = $18,000 Annual depreciation = $18,000 ÷ 3 = $6,000 Rate = $6,000 ÷ $40,000 × 100 = 15% → Answer: B
Q21 2021 HSC ↗ 2 marks

Adam purchased some office furniture five years ago. It depreciated by $2300 each year based on the straight-line method of depreciation. The salvage value of the furniture is now $7500. Find the initial value of the office furniture.

Show solution
Total depreciation = 5 × $2300 = $11,500 Initial value = Salvage value + Total depreciation = $7500 + $11,500 = $19,000
Q22 SM-Bank 3 marks

A company purchased a machine for $60,000. For taxation purposes the machine is depreciated using the straight-line method at a flat rate of 10% of the purchase price each year.

(i) By how many dollars will the machine depreciate annually? [1 mark]

(ii) Calculate the value of the machine after three years. [1 mark]

(iii) After how many years will the machine be $12,000 in value? [1 mark]

Show solution
(i) Annual depreciation = 10% × $60,000 = $6,000 (ii) Value after 3 years = $60,000 − 3 × $6,000 = $60,000 − $18,000 = $42,000 (iii) $12,000 = $60,000 − $6,000 × n $6,000n = $48,000 → n = 8 = 8 years
