Section 1

Data Classification

Q1 2018 HSC 1 mark

A survey asked the following question: "How many brothers do you have?" How would the responses be classified?

  • A. Categorical, ordinal
  • B. Categorical, nominal
  • C. Numerical, discrete
  • D. Numerical, continuous
Show solution

"Number of brothers" is a count — it can only take whole number values. It is numerical and discrete.

Answer: C
Q2 2015 HSC 1 mark

A survey asked the following question for students born in Australia: "Which State or Territory were you born in?" How would the responses be classified?

  • A. Categorical, ordinal
  • B. Categorical, nominal
  • C. Numerical, discrete
  • D. Numerical, continuous
Show solution

State/Territory names are categories with no natural ordering (you can't say one state is "greater than" another).

Answer: B — Categorical, nominal
Q3 2009 HSC 1 mark

The eye colours of a sample of children were recorded. When analysing this data, which of the following could be found?

  • A. Mean
  • B. Median
  • C. Mode
  • D. Range
Show solution

Eye colour is categorical/nominal data. You cannot calculate a mean, median, or range for categories, but you can find the most common (mode).

Answer: C — Mode
Q4 2015 HSC 1 mark

On a school report, a student's record of completing homework is graded using the following codes: C = consistently, U = usually, S = sometimes, R = rarely, N = never. What type of data is this?

  • A. Categorical, ordinal
  • B. Categorical, nominal
  • C. Numerical, continuous
  • D. Numerical, discrete
Show solution

The categories (C, U, S, R, N) have a clear natural order from most to least frequent. This is categorical ordinal data.

Answer: A — Categorical, ordinal
Q5 2016 HSC 1 mark

Which set of data is classified as categorical and nominal?

  • A. blue, green, yellow
  • B. small, medium, large
  • C. 5.2 cm, 6 cm, 7.21 cm
  • D. 4 people, 5 people, 9 people
Show solution

Colours (blue, green, yellow) are categories with no natural ordering — they are categorical nominal. "Small, medium, large" has an order, so it would be ordinal.

Answer: A
Q24 SM-Bank 1 mark

In a survey, Reuben is asked to give some information about himself. The answers to which of the following questions would provide data which is quantitative and discrete?

  • A. Eye colour
  • B. Gender (M or F)
  • C. Height
  • D. Number of siblings
Show solution

Quantitative data involves numbers. Discrete data are whole-number counts. "Number of siblings" is counted, so it is quantitative discrete.

Answer: D
Q25 SM-Bank 1 mark

Alex works in an electrical store and asks each customer who purchases an item their postcode. Which type of data is Alex collecting?

  • A. Categorical Ordinal
  • B. Categorical Nominal
  • C. Quantitative Continuous
  • D. Quantitative Discrete
Show solution

A postcode is a number but is not counted or measured — it identifies a location. It implies no natural ordering, so it is Categorical Nominal.

Answer: B
Q26 SM-Bank 1 mark

Leica collects information from the members of her netball team in order to order uniforms. Which is an example of ordinal categorical data?

  • A. Their waist measurements in mm
  • B. Their height in cm
  • C. The colour of shirts they want
  • D. Their shirt sizes (S, M, L, XL…)
Show solution

Shirt sizes are labels (names), so they are categorical. They also imply an order (S < M < L < XL), so they are ordinal. Measurements (A and B) are quantitative continuous.

Answer: D

Sampling Methods & Statistical Inquiry

Q6 2013 HSC 1 mark

A high school has 100 students in each year group, Year 7 to Year 12. A survey is to be conducted to determine the average number of text messages sent per month by students at the school. Which of the following would provide the most representative sample for this survey?

  • A. All Year 7 students
  • B. All physics students in Year 11 and 12
  • C. 20 students chosen at random from each year group
  • D. 120 students chosen at random from the school roll
Show solution

Option C is a stratified sample — it gives each year group equal representation, providing the most representative sample across the school.

Answer: C

Selecting every nth item is systematic sampling.

Q19 2019 HSC 3 marks

The heights, in centimetres, of 10 players on a basketball team are shown below:

170, 180, 185, 188, 192, 193, 193, 194, 196, 202

Is the height of the shortest player on the team considered an outlier? Justify your answer with calculations.

Show solution
Lower half: 170, 180, 185, 188, 192 → Q1 = 185 Upper half: 193, 193, 194, 196, 202 → Q3 = 194 IQR = Q3 − Q1 = 194 − 185 = 9 Lower fence = Q1 − 1.5 × IQR = 185 − 13.5 = 171.5 Shortest player = 170 cm 170 < 171.5 Yes, 170 cm IS an outlier (it is below the lower fence of 171.5).
