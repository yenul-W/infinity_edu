Section 1

Wages, Overtime & Allowances

Q1 2009 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Billy worked for 35 hours at the normal hourly rate of pay and for five hours at double time. He earned $561.60 in total for this work. What was the normal hourly rate of pay?

  • A. $7.02
  • B. $12.48
  • C. $14.04
  • D. $16.05
Show solution
Let normal rate = r 35r + 5 × (2r) = 561.60 35r + 10r = 561.60 → 45r = 561.60 r = $12.48 → Answer: B
Q2 2008 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Luke's normal rate of pay is $15 per hour. Last week he was paid for 12 hours, at time-and-a-half. How many hours would Luke need to work this week, at double time, to earn the same amount?

  • A. 4
  • B. 6
  • C. 8
  • D. 9
Show solution
Last week earnings = 12 × $15 × 1.5 = $270 Double time rate = $15 × 2 = $30/hr Hours needed = $270 ÷ $30 = 9 hours → Answer: D
Q3 2017 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Tom earns a weekly wage of $1025. He also receives an additional allowance of $87.50 per day when handling toxic substances. What is Tom's income in a fortnight in which he handles toxic substances on 5 separate days?

  • A. $1112.50
  • B. $1462.50
  • C. $2225.00
  • D. $2487.50
Show solution
Fortnightly wage = 2 × $1025 = $2050 Toxic allowance = 5 × $87.50 = $437.50 Total = $2050 + $437.50 = $2487.50 → Answer: D
Q4 2022 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Tian is paid $20.45 per hour, as well as a meal allowance of $16.20 per day. What are Tian's total earnings if she works 9 hours per day for 5 days?

Show solution
Hourly pay = $20.45 × 9 × 5 = $920.25 Meal allowance = $16.20 × 5 = $81.00 Total = $920.25 + $81.00 = $1001.25
Q5 2013 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Luke's normal rate of pay is $24.80 per hour. In one week he worked 14 hours at the normal rate, 4 hours at time-and-a-half, and 3.5 hours at double time. He was also paid a wet weather allowance of $50 for the week. What was his pay for the week?

  • A. $583.20
  • B. $620.40
  • C. $669.60
  • D. $719.60
Show solution
Normal: 14 × $24.80 = $347.20 Time-and-a-half: 4 × $24.80 × 1.5 = $148.80 Double time: 3.5 × $24.80 × 2 = $173.60 Allowance: $50.00 Total = 347.20 + 148.80 + 173.60 + 50 = $719.60 → Answer: D
Q6 2016 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Isabella works a 35-hour week and is paid at an hourly rate of $18. Any overtime hours worked are paid at time-and-a-half. In a particular week, she earned $1008. How many hours in total did Isabella work in this week?

  • A. 37.3
  • B. 42
  • C. 49
  • D. 56
Show solution
Normal pay = 35 × $18 = $630 Overtime pay = $1008 − $630 = $378 OT rate = $18 × 1.5 = $27/hr OT hours = $378 ÷ $27 = 14 hours Total = 35 + 14 = 49 hours → Answer: C

Section 2

Commission, Allowances & Leave Loading

Q7 2014 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Jane sells jewellery. Her commission is based on a sliding scale of 6% on the first $2000 of her sales, 3.5% on the next $1000, and 2% thereafter. What is Jane's commission when her total sales are $5670?

  • A. $188.40
  • B. $208.40
  • C. $321.85
  • D. $652.05
Show solution
First $2000: 6% × $2000 = $120.00 Next $1000: 3.5% × $1000 = $35.00 Remaining ($5670 − $3000 = $2670): 2% × $2670 = $53.40 Total = $120.00 + $35.00 + $53.40 = $208.40 → Answer: B
Q8 2006 HSC ↗ 1 mark

A salesman earns $200 per week plus $40 commission for each item he sells. How many items does he need to sell to earn a total of $2640 in two weeks?

  • A. 33
  • B. 56
  • C. 61
  • D. 66
Show solution
Base pay (2 weeks) = 2 × $200 = $400 Commission needed = $2640 − $400 = $2240 Items = $2240 ÷ $40 = 56 items → Answer: B
Q9 2007 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Joe is about to go on holidays for four weeks. His weekly salary is $280 and his holiday loading is 17.5% of four weeks pay. What is Joe's total pay for the four weeks holiday?

  • A. $196
  • B. $329
  • C. $1169
  • D. $1316
Show solution
Four weeks pay = 4 × $280 = $1120 Leave loading = 17.5% × $1120 = $196 Total = $1120 + $196 = $1316 → Answer: D
Q10 2016 HSC ↗ 3 marks

Jenny earns a yearly salary of $63,752. Her annual leave loading is 17.5% of four weeks pay. Calculate her total pay for her four weeks of annual leave.

Show solution
Weekly pay = $63,752 ÷ 52 = $1226 (approx) Four weeks pay = 4 × $1226 = $4904 Leave loading = 17.5% × $4904 = $858.20 Total = $4904 + $858.20 = $5762.20
Q11 2022 HSC ↗ 2 marks

A real estate agent's commission for selling houses is 2% for the first $800,000 of the sale price and 1.5% for any amount over $800,000. Calculate the commission earned in selling a house for $1,500,000.

Show solution
First $800,000: 2% × $800,000 = $16,000 Over $800,000: $1,500,000 − $800,000 = $700,000 1.5% × $700,000 = $10,500 Total commission = $16,000 + $10,500 = $26,500

Section 3

Net Pay & Deductions

Q12 2015 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Gayle's gross pay each week is $952.25. The following deductions are taken from her gross pay each week: tax $180.93, superannuation $85.70, union membership $21.40, health fund $38.15. What is Gayle's net pay each week?

  • A. $326.18
  • B. $626.07
  • C. $771.32
  • D. $952.25
Show solution
Total deductions = 180.93 + 85.70 + 21.40 + 38.15 = $326.18 Net pay = $952.25 − $326.18 = $626.07 → Answer: B
Q13 2018 HSC ↗ 1 mark

David earns a gross income of $890 per week. Each week, 25% of this income is deducted in taxation. David budgets to save 20% of his net income. How much does he budget to save each week?

  • A. $44.50
  • B. $133.50
  • C. $489.50
  • D. $534.00
Show solution
Tax = 25% × $890 = $222.50 Net income = $890 − $222.50 = $667.50 Savings = 20% × $667.50 = $133.50 → Answer: B
Q14 2012 HSC ↗ 3 marks

Tai earns a gross weekly wage of $1024. Each week her deductions are: tax instalment of $296.40, health fund contribution of $24.50, union fees of $15.80. She also pays $3640 over the year as her share of household expenses. What percentage of her net wage does Tai pay for household expenses?

Show solution
Net weekly wage = $1024 − $296.40 − $24.50 − $15.80 = $687.30 Annual net wage = $687.30 × 52 = $35,739.60 Percentage = ($3640 ÷ $35,739.60) × 100 ≈ 10.2%

Section 4

Taxation

Q15 2011 HSC ↗ 3 marks

Sri has a gross salary of $56,350. She has tax deductions of $350 for union fees, $2000 in work-related expenses and $250 in donations to charities. The Medicare levy is 1.5% of her taxable income. Calculate Sri's Medicare levy.

Show solution
Taxable income = $56,350 − $350 − $2000 − $250 = $53,750 Medicare levy = 1.5% × $53,750 = $806.25
Q16 2016 HSC ↗ 5 marks

Theo is completing his tax return. He has a gross salary of $82,521 and income from a rental property totalling $10,920. He is claiming $13,420 in allowable deductions.

Taxable incomeTax payable
$0 – $18,200Nil
$18,201 – $37,00019c for each $1 over $18,200
$37,001 – $87,000$3,572 + 32.5c for each $1 over $37,000
$87,001 – $180,000$19,822 + 37c for each $1 over $87,000
$180,001 and over$54,232 + 45c for each $1 over $180,000

(i) Determine Theo's taxable income. [1 mark]

(ii) Using the tax table, calculate Theo's tax payable. [2 marks]

(iii) Theo must also pay a Medicare levy of $1600.42. He has already paid $20,525 as Pay As You Go (PAYG) tax. Should Theo receive a tax refund or will he owe more tax? Justify with calculations. [2 marks]

Show solution
(i) Taxable income = $82,521 + $10,920 − $13,420 = $80,021 (ii) Tax = $3,572 + 32.5% × ($80,021 − $37,000) = $3,572 + 0.325 × $43,021 = $3,572 + $13,981.83 ≈ $17,553.83 (iii) Total owed = $17,553.83 + $1,600.42 = $19,154.25 PAYG paid = $20,525.00 Refund = $20,525 − $19,154.25 = $1,370.75 (Theo receives a refund)

Section 5

Medicare Levy & Net Income

Q18 2005 HSC ↗ 1 mark

Janet's gross income last year was $60,000. She had allowable tax deductions of $5000. Janet paid 1.5% of her taxable income for the Medicare levy. How much was Janet's Medicare levy?

  • A. $750
  • B. $825
  • C. $900
  • D. $975
Show solution
Taxable income = $60,000 − $5000 = $55,000 Medicare levy = 1.5% × $55,000 = $825 → Answer: B
